Comparisons:


Comparisons are used to evaluate whether two values are equal, not equal, greater than, less than, etc.
Common comparison operators include:

Equal to (==)
Not equal to (!=)
Greater than (>)
Less than (<)
Greater than or equal to (>=)
Less than or equal to (<=)

Comparisons usually result in a boolean value (true or false) depending on whether the condition is met.




Logical Operators:


Logical operators are used to combine multiple conditions and evaluate them together.
Common logical operators include:

AND (&& or 'and' in Python)
OR (|| or 'or' in Python)
NOT (! or 'not' in Python)

Logical operators operate on boolean values and produce boolean results.

AND returns true only if both conditions are true.
OR returns true if at least one condition is true.
NOT negates the result of the condition.
