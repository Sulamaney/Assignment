DIFFERENCES BTW LET, VAR AND CONST


Scope:
var: Variables declared with var are function-scoped or globally scoped. They are hoisted to the top of their containing function or script.

let and const: Variables declared with let and const are block-scoped. They are only accessible within the block (enclosed by {}) in which they are defined.

Hoisting:
var: Variables declared with var are hoisted to the top of their containing function or script. This means you can use a variable before
it's declared without causing an error, although its value will be undefined.

let and const: Variables declared with let and const are also hoisted, but they are not initialized until their declaration is evaluated.
Accessing them before the declaration results in a ReferenceError.

Reassignment:
var and let: Variables declared with var and let can be reassigned new values.

const: Variables declared with const cannot be reassigned after they have been initialized. However, for objects and arrays declared with const,
the properties or elements of the object or array can be modified.

Temporal Dead Zone (TDZ):
let and const: Variables declared with let and const are in the Temporal Dead Zone (TDZ) from the start of the block until the declaration is encountered.
Accessing them during this time results in a ReferenceError.

var: Variables declared with var do not have a TDZ because they are initialized with the value undefined during hoisting.


SIMILARITIES BTW LET, VAR AND CONST


Declaration Syntax:
All three (let, var, and const) are used for variable declaration.

They are all followed by the variable name and an optional initializer (value assigned to the variable at the time of declaration).

Global Scope:
When declared outside of any function, all three variables (let, var, and const) become properties of the global object (window in browsers).

Lexical Scoping:
They all follow lexical scoping rules, meaning they are resolved in the scope in which they are defined, not where they are executed.
