EXPLAINATION OF FOR, WHILE, DO WHILE LOOP


for loop:
Best for: When you know the exact number of iterations needed.
Example: Iterating over an array, performing a fixed number of iterations, or iterating over a range of numbers.

while loop:
Best for: When you need to loop until a condition becomes false, but you don't know in advance how many iterations will be required.

Example: Performing input validation until the user enters valid input, or continuously polling for updates until a certain condition is met.

do...while loop:
Best for: Similar to the while loop, but ensures that the loop body is executed at least once before checking the condition.

Example: Menu-driven programs where the user has to perform at least one action before quitting, or situations where you need to execute a block of code at least once regardless of the condition.
